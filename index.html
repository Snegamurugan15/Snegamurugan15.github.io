👤 About Me

Statistician by training  ·  Data engineer by practice
Analyst by instinct — turning complex information into decisions that matter.

I am a Big Data Analyst with a background that spans statistical modelling, data engineering, and machine learning — disciplines I have always found most useful when they work together rather than in isolation. My foundation is in Statistics, built through years studying inference, econometrics, and time-series analysis, which shaped how I think about data before I ever touch a tool.

The most formative part of my career was at the Reserve Bank of India, where I worked inside the Foreign Exchange Department on live macroeconomic datasets at a scale I had not encountered before. Working in a regulated environment, building validation pipelines, and presenting findings to senior leadership gave me a clear sense of what responsible, high-impact data work actually looks like. I then deliberately expanded into Big Data Analytics at Lambton College — adding distributed computing, cloud platforms, ML pipelines, and real-time data processing.

I am genuinely interested in how AI is reshaping analytics — from generative tools that accelerate exploratory work to LLMs that are changing how we interact with data entirely. I follow this space closely and actively incorporate these tools into how I work day to day. Currently based in Canada on a Post-Graduation Work Permit and actively seeking Data Analyst and Big Data Analyst roles.

    Unlocking FDI Trends — Reserve Bank of India

    Institutional research for India's central bank: a three-part study predicting national FDI inflows using ML classifiers, analysing Tamil Nadu sector-wise FDI trends, and modelling long-run FDI–GVA relationships using econometric methods across Manufacturing, Services, Electricity, Construction, and Mining.

    📈Achieved 83% SVM prediction accuracy — closest to actual 2023 inflows of ₹36,74,350M — by comparing 5 ML classifiers against 5 international index scores; confirmed bidirectional causality in 4 of 5 sectors; certified and presented to senior management at RBI Chennai.
    Technologies Used
    RSVMRandom ForestXGBoostGLMRidge RegressionVECMJohansen CointegrationGranger CausalityIRFVIFggplot2dplyr
    View on GitHub →

    The Problem

    India's central bank needed a systematic approach to predicting national FDI inflows and understanding how foreign investment relates to economic output — with limited automated forecasting capability across 5 economic sectors.

    My Approach

    Three-part analysis: (1) compared 5 ML classifiers using international index scores (EoDB, FDICI, GII, IIP, IEF); (2) Tamil Nadu sector-wise FDI trend analysis; (3) Johansen Cointegration + VECM + Granger Causality + IRF to model long-run FDI–GVA relationships. VIF used for multicollinearity validation.

    The Outcome

    SVM at 83% accuracy — closest to actual ₹36,74,350M. Bidirectional causality confirmed in 4 of 5 sectors. 5-quarter FDI and GVA forecasts generated using VECM and Vec2VAR models. Research certified by RBI Chennai senior management.

    DrivenInsights — Used Car Price Prediction (USA)

    End-to-end big data ML pipeline on 3,000,040 U.S. used car records (66 raw columns) to understand pricing dynamics and build a predictive model. Covers feature engineering, distributed PySpark processing, model comparison, and animated visualisations for business stakeholders.

    📈Reduced RMSE to 1.135 (Random Forest) — outperforming Linear Regression (4.89) and GBT (28.44) — by narrowing 66 features to 12 key predictors and training on 3M+ records; identified Sedans as most price-stable for U.S. families.
    Technologies Used
    PythonPySparkSparkSessionVectorAssemblerRandom ForestLinear RegressionGradient Boosted TreesRegressionEvaluatorPlotlySeabornpandasNumPy
    View on GitHub →

    The Problem

    U.S. families increasingly rely on used vehicles due to rising new-car costs, but pricing is opaque and variable. The challenge: analyse a 3,000,040-record dataset with 66 columns to identify pricing drivers and build a reliable prediction model.

    My Approach

    Feature selection: 66 → 12 key predictors (mileage, year, seating capacity, fuel economy, dimensions). PySpark ML pipeline with SparkSession, VectorAssembler, 80/20 split.     RF RMSE 1.135 (best); SUV/Crossover peaked at 250K listings in 2020; Sedans and Hatchbacks most price-stable. Nissan Altima, Toyota Corolla, and Chevrolet Malibu flagged as top family-value vehicles.
